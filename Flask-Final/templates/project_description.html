Data cleaning Process:

Data cleaning is a crucial step in the data analysis process, ensuring that the data is accurate, consistent, and reliable for further analysis and modeling. The process typically involves several steps, starting from data collection and ending with the transformation of the cleaned data into a suitable format for analysis. Here's an outline of the data cleaning process step by step:

  1. Data Collection: Data collection is the initial step in our data cleaning process, involving gathering data from the web. We collected structured data from web pages.
  2. Data Inspection: After collecting the data, our next step is to inspect it thoroughly to identify any inconsistencies, errors, missing values, or outliers. This involves examining the data's structure, format, and quality to gain insights into its overall cleanliness and validity.
  3. Handling Missing Values: Missing values are a common issue in real-world datasets and need to be addressed before proceeding with the analysis. Depending on the extent of missingness and the nature of the data, we handle missing values through various techniques such as imputation, deletion, or flagging.
  4. Dealing with Duplicates: Duplicate records or entries in the dataset can skew the analysis results and should be identified and removed. Duplicate detection involves comparing rows or records based on certain criteria and identifying exact or near-duplicate entries.
  5. Data Transformation: Data transformation involves converting the data into a suitable format for analysis, which may include standardizing units of measurement, encoding categorical variables, or normalizing numerical values.
  6. Handling Outliers: Outliers are data points that deviate significantly from the rest of the dataset and can have a disproportionate impact on statistical analyses. Identifying and handling outliers involves determining their presence using statistical methods and deciding whether to remove, transform, or retain them.
  7. Data Integration and Validation: After cleaning and transforming the datasets, they may need to be integrated or combined to create a unified dataset for analysis. Data integration involves merging or joining datasets based on common identifiers or keys.
  8. Documentation and Reporting: Throughout our data cleaning process, it's essential to maintain detailed documentation of the steps taken, including any modifications, transformations, or decisions made regarding the data. Reporting the findings of our data cleaning process, along with any insights gained and decisions made, provides valuable context for subsequent analyses and interpretations.
